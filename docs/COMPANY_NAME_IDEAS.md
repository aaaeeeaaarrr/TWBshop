# Company / product name — shortlist for the owner to pick (NOT decided)

You asked for "something catchy, easy abbreviation." Here's a curated shortlist for the product = a
**multi-tenant, config-driven, total business-management platform** (attendance · POS · stock · back-office
· more), channel-agnostic, sold as a service, starting in Cambodia/SE-Asia. **I did not choose — pick one
(or mix), then we check trademark + .com before committing.**

## Naming principles I used
- Short (1–3 syllables), easy to say + abbreviate, pronounceable for Khmer + English speakers.
- Brandable (a coined word usually beats a generic one — easier trademark + .com).
- Hints at "operations / all-in-one / the brain," without boxing us into ONE feature (we're not "an
  attendance app").
- Avoid an obvious existing-brand clash (flagged where I suspect one).

## Top picks (coined / brandable — best for trademark + domain)
| Name | Why | Abbr / handle | Tagline idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Opero** | Latin "I work / I operate" — professional, clean, global. | OPR · @opero | "Run the whole shop." |
| **Tenra** | Strong, abstract; quiet nod to multi-**ten**ant. | TNR · @tenra | "One system, every branch." |
| **Corevo** | **Core** + evolve — the brain that grows with you. | CRV · @corevo | "Your business core." |
| **Hivory** | **Hive** (coordinated workers) + a premium ring. | HIV · @hivory | "The hive that runs itself." |
| **Kaden** | Operational **cadence** / rhythm; soft, brandable. | KDN · @kaden | "Keep the rhythm." |
| **Opsi** | "**Ops**" + friendly modern-SaaS ending. | OPS · @opsi | "Operations, sorted." |
